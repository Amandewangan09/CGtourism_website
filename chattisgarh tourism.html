Why Visit Chhattisgarh?

From breathtaking waterfalls to ancient caves, Chhattisgarh offers unique experiences for every traveler

Natural Beauty

Home to stunning waterfalls like Chitrakote and Tirathgarh, and lush national parks

Spiritual Heritage

Ancient temples like Bhoramdeo and Danteshwari showcase rich architectural heritage

Tribal Culture

Experience vibrant tribal traditions, dances, and crafts of indigenous communities

Popular Destinations

Explore these must-visit places in Chhattisgarh

Chitrakote Waterfalls

Known as the "Niagara of India", this horseshoe-shaped waterfall is breathtaking during monsoon.

Bastar District

Bhoramdeo Temple

Often called the "Khajuraho of Chhattisgarh", this 11th century temple complex features exquisite carvings.

Kabirdham District

Kanger Valley National Park

A biodiversity hotspot with limestone caves, waterfalls, and rich wildlife including the state bird, Bastar Hill Myna.

Bastar District

Sirpur Heritage Site

An important archaeological site with ancient Buddhist, Hindu and Jain monuments dating back to 5th-8th century.

Mahasamund District

Barnawapara Wildlife Sanctuary

Home to tigers, leopards, sloth bears, and various deer species in a pristine forest environment.

Mahasamund District

Danteshwari Temple

One of the 52 Shakti Peethas, this ancient temple in Dantewada is a major pilgrimage site.

Dantewada District

Unique Experiences

Immerse yourself in the authentic culture of Chhattisgarh

Tribal Village Tours

Experience the daily life of indigenous tribes like Gond, Baiga and Maria through guided village visits. Learn about their traditional farming techniques, handicrafts, and folk arts.

  • Participate in traditional dance performances
  • Learn tribal cooking methods
  • Witness craft-making demonstrations

Jungle Safari & Wildlife

Explore Chhattisgarh's rich biodiversity through guided safaris in its national parks and wildlife sanctuaries. Spot tigers, leopards, wild buffalo and diverse bird species.

  • Night safaris in Achanakmar Tiger Reserve
  • Bird watching in Guru Ghasidas National Park
  • Nature walks with tribal guides
